1) Gap Identification & Prioritization

Key unresolved medical questions, inactive workstreams, and partially addressed gaps.

Nature of Unresolved Medical Questions New
  • Across lung and breast cancers, the dominant uncertainties center on how to position abecma-based regimens across the continuum—monotherapy versus combinations, induction/neoadjuvant, maintenance, and perioperative relative to current standards.
  • Biomarker segmentation remains insufficiently defined, including PD-L1/CPS strata, STK11/KEAP1, ER/HER2-low, and outcomes in high-risk special populations such as brain or liver metastases, poor performance status, and bleeding risk.
  • Novel ADC or bispecific combinations still lack comparative safety-efficacy profiles, constraining optimization of multi-agent strategies.

Landscape Breadth
  • Across solid tumors, the PD-L1 × VEGF concept is being explored where immune exclusion and angiogenic signaling co-dominate the microenvironment.
  • Emerging relevance appears most concentrated in vascularized, myeloid-inflamed settings that historically use checkpoint inhibitors paired with anti-angiogenics.
Treatment Paradigm Fit
  • The asset aligns with practice patterns that pair checkpoint blockade with anti-angiogenic therapy while consolidating dual pathway modulation into a single molecule.
  • It fits best where normalizing vasculature and restoring effector function are jointly sought.
Signal Concentration Drivers
  • Promise tends to emerge in immune-excluded, hypoxic tumors with high angiogenesis signatures where VEGF-driven endothelial dysfunction impairs T-cell entry.

3) Competitive Benchmarking

Comprehensive competitor overview, comparator assets, and readout structure.

Key benchmark summary
  • Abecma’s mechanism and positioning should be benchmarked against direct comparator assets with ongoing programs and overlapping population definitions.
  • Competitive differentiation is likely to depend on biomarker precision, combination safety, and evidence timing.
Recommended focus
  • Track company, MoA, phase/status, population inclusion/exclusion, endpoints, and duration as the core comparison framework.
  • Use the benchmark to identify where the BMS asset can claim stronger clinical, operational, or scientific differentiation.

4) Asset Strategy

Strategic imperatives, EGOs, and underlying key data maps.

Strategic imperatives
  • Advance the science in progressive fibrotic lung diseases, including IPF and PPF.
  • Educate the community on the drug pathway and phase 2 results.
  • Engage with external experts and form strong partnerships.
Execution focus
  • Increase disease awareness, differentiate LPA1 MoA, demonstrate long-term efficacy, establish safety and tolerability, and drive early value.

Evidence Generation Objectives (EGOs)
− Collapse
EGO 1

Increase IPF & PPF awareness

  • Establish parity in disease burden between IPF & PPF to address HCP perceptions on differing severities & generate treatment inertia
  • Highlight the need for timely diagnosis & early treatment initiation in IPF & PPF
  • Drive awareness into real-world treatment patterns & limitations associated with current Tx options
EGO 2

Differentiate LPA1 MoA

  • Differentiate & story build vs ever-evolving competitive landscape within IPF & PPF
  • Establish antifibrotic halo within non-pulmonary fibrotic diseases
  • Identify prognostic circulating & imaging biomarkers implicated within LPA1 pathway
EGO 3

Demonstrate long-term efficacy

  • Demonstrate meaningful disease-modification of long-term outcomes, quality of life, functional measures, & survival
  • Generate evidence in special populations excluded in Ph3
EGO 4

Establish safety & tolerability

  • Demonstrate favorable safety & tolerability profile, including AEs, DDIs, adherence, & (re)titration schemes
  • Generate evidence in special populations excluded in Phase 3
EGO 5

Drive early value

  • Develop a strong value proposition for payers to show differentiated benefit
  • Establish positioning in Tx paradigm including monotherapy, combination therapy, & switches
